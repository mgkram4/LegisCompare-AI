Education Equity and Access Act of 2024 - Revised Version

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE
This Act may be cited as the "Enhanced Education Equity and Access Act of 2024".

SECTION 2. FINDINGS AND PURPOSE
Congress finds that:
(1) Educational disparities persist across different communities, economic backgrounds, and geographic regions.
(2) Access to quality education is fundamental to economic mobility, democratic participation, and national competitiveness.
(3) Federal investment in education infrastructure and personnel is necessary to ensure equal opportunities for all students.
(4) Technology integration is essential for modern educational excellence.

SECTION 3. EDUCATION FUNDING FORMULA
(a) BASIC ALLOCATION - The Secretary shall allocate funds to eligible school districts based on:
(1) Student enrollment numbers
(2) Percentage of students eligible for free or reduced-price lunch
(3) Geographic cost adjustments
(4) Special needs population percentage
(5) English language learner population

(b) MINIMUM FUNDING - No school district shall receive less than $10,500 per student annually, adjusted for regional cost differences.

(c) PERFORMANCE INCENTIVES - Additional funding of up to 15% may be awarded based on:
(1) Student achievement improvements
(2) Graduation rate increases
(3) College readiness metrics

SECTION 4. TEACHER SUPPORT PROGRAMS
(a) TEACHER TRAINING - The Secretary shall establish comprehensive programs to provide:
(1) Professional development opportunities including STEM and digital literacy training
(2) Mentorship programs for new teachers with dedicated support coordinators
(3) Technology training and ongoing technical support
(4) Cultural competency and inclusive education training

(b) RETENTION INCENTIVES - Eligible teachers may receive:
(1) Student loan forgiveness up to $25,000 for teachers in high-need schools
(2) Housing assistance including down payment support in high-need areas
(3) Professional development stipends up to $3,000 annually
(4) Health and wellness benefits enhancement

(c) RECRUITMENT INITIATIVES - Programs to attract high-quality teachers including:
(1) Scholarship programs for education majors
(2) Alternative certification pathways for career changers
(3) Relocation assistance for teachers moving to underserved areas

SECTION 5. INFRASTRUCTURE AND TECHNOLOGY
The Secretary shall prioritize funding for:
(1) Building repairs, modernization, and climate-resilient improvements
(2) Comprehensive technology infrastructure including high-speed internet
(3) Digital devices and software for all students
(4) Enhanced library and laboratory facilities with modern equipment
(5) Safe and efficient transportation systems including electric vehicle transition
(6) Accessibility improvements for students with disabilities

SECTION 6. STUDENT SUPPORT SERVICES
(a) WRAPAROUND SERVICES - Funding for:
(1) Mental health counselors and social workers
(2) After-school and summer learning programs
(3) Nutrition programs and food security initiatives
(4) Family engagement coordinators

(b) COLLEGE AND CAREER READINESS - Support for:
(1) Advanced Placement and dual enrollment programs
(2) Career and technical education pathways
(3) College counseling and application assistance
(4) Internship and apprenticeship opportunities

SECTION 7. ACCOUNTABILITY AND TRANSPARENCY
(a) Enhanced reporting requirements including quarterly financial reports
(b) Student performance tracking with disaggregated data by demographic groups
(c) Community engagement requirements with mandatory public hearings
(d) Independent oversight committee with community representation
(e) Annual third-party audits of fund usage and effectiveness

SECTION 8. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS
There are authorized to be appropriated $18.5 billion for fiscal year 2025, and such sums as may be necessary for each of the 6 succeeding fiscal years, with automatic inflation adjustments.

SECTION 9. IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE
(a) Phase 1: Infrastructure and immediate needs funding available January 1, 2025
(b) Phase 2: Teacher support programs begin July 1, 2025
(c) Phase 3: Full implementation by September 1, 2025

SECTION 10. EFFECTIVE DATE
This Act shall take effect on January 1, 2025, with early implementation provisions taking effect 60 days after enactment.
