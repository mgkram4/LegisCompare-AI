ENHANCED EDUCATION EQUITY AND ACCESS ACT

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
This Act may be cited as the "Enhanced Education Equity and Access Act".

SECTION 2. FINDINGS AND PURPOSE.
(a) FINDINGS.—Congress finds that—
(1) educational disparities persist across socioeconomic and geographic lines;
(2) rural and urban Title I schools lack adequate resources for effective education;
(3) digital access gaps limit student opportunities beginning in elementary grades.

SECTION 3. FUNDING AUTHORIZATION.
(a) IN GENERAL.—There is authorized to be appropriated $2,000,000,000 annually for each of fiscal years 2024 through 2029 to carry out this Act.
(b) ALLOCATION.—Funds shall be allocated to Title I schools and rural educational agencies based on student enrollment and need assessment.

SECTION 4. DIGITAL ACCESS PROGRAM.
(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—The Secretary shall establish a digital access program to provide technology resources to students in grades 3 through 12.
(b) ELIGIBLE STUDENTS.—Students in Title I schools and rural districts shall be eligible for laptops, tablets, and internet access under this program.

SECTION 5. TEACHER SUPPORT.
(a) PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT.—The Secretary shall provide professional development programs for teachers in participating schools.
(b) TRAINING REQUIREMENTS.—Teachers must complete 40 hours of annual training to maintain program eligibility.
(c) ACCOUNTABILITY MEASURES.—Schools must implement enhanced assessment and reporting requirements to track student progress.
